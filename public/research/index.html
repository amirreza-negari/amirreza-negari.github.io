Research

Research Directions

My work sits between quantum information, quantum matter, and error correction. The directions below group my papers by the questions they are trying to answer.

Direction 01

Mixed-state phases and quantum memories

I study noisy quantum memories as many-body mixed states. A recurring theme is that local observables can look short-ranged while information-theoretic quantities, such as conditional mutual information and Markov length, reveal hidden long-range structure relevant for fault tolerance.

Related papers Critical non-equilibrium phases from noisy topological memories Spacetime Markov length: a diagnostic for fault tolerance via mixed-state phases Measurement-induced phase transitions in the toric code

Direction 02

Entanglement theory and symmetry

I am interested in how symmetry and superselection constraints shape entanglement, separability, and information-processing tasks in many-body systems, especially in regimes where standard pure-state intuition is no longer enough.

Related papers Symmetry enforces entanglement at high temperatures Extendibility of Fermionic Gaussian States

Direction 03

Learnability and decodability

I study when physical information can be learned, decoded, or represented efficiently from accessible data. This includes phases that are hard to learn from local data and diagnostics for when quantum memories admit local versus global recovery.

Related papers Unlearnable phases of matter Spacetime Markov length: a diagnostic for fault tolerance via mixed-state phases

Direction 04

Quantum many-body numerics

I also work on numerical approaches to strongly correlated quantum systems, including quantum Monte Carlo, fermionic criticality, and methods aimed at mitigating sign-problem obstructions.

Related papers Chiral Ising Gross-Neveu criticality of a single Dirac cone An Amelioration for the Sign Problem: Adiabatic Quantum Monte Carlo
